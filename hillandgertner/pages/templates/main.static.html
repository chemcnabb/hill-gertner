a talent-based merchant bank

Hill & Gertner Capital Corporation (Hill & Gertner) is a talent-based merchant bank headquartered in Toronto. We invest our own capital and that of third parties, and form partnerships with proven creative entrepreneurs who have extensive expertise in their respective fields. We are an agile organization driven by our belief in the entrepreneurial spirit, our ability to recognize new opportunities, and our long-standing personal relationships across all industries.

At Hill & Gertner, we embrace change and disruption, believing that it is a natural by-product of progress. When we see change, we see opportunity. More than this, we have the knowledge, know-how and financial resources to capitalize on the ever-evolving business environment. We work with retailers, manufacturers, distributors and service providers whose businesses are being affected by expansion, re-tooling, a business climate in flux, and mergers or acquisitions. Moreover, we are not intimidated by financial distress. Our strength is in our ability to act quickly and deploy our expertise and capital to maximum effect.

Hill & Gertner Retail Partners (HGRP)

Hill & Gernter Retail Partners (HGRP) is an investment banking and advisory firm specializing in the merchandising sector, including retail and consumer goods. HGRP are also principals and equity investors in successful retail and consumer product companies. HGRP’s services include branding and rebranding mergers and acquisitions, capital solutions, consolidations, restructuring, real estate solutions, and advisory services.

HGRP’s expertise in branding, merchandising, consolidations, restructuring and banking means that it understands a company’s potential and can maximize that value in any kind of transaction. More than any other company in Canada, HGRP knows what it takes to be a successful retailer. Since 1996, HGRP has been involved in retail consumer product transactions totaling well over $1 billion.

HGRP’s principals have visionary, branding, strategic, merchandising and operational skills that are unmatched in the Canadian market. These skills are complemented by an expertise in financial engineering, all of which enables HGRP to provide creative turnkey solutions.

Quadrangle Architects Limited

Since its formation in 1986, Quadrangle Architects Limited has established itself as one of Canada’s most dynamic architectural firms. The practice was formed by the merger of Klein Taylor Goldsmith Limited (established in 1978) and Curtner Brown Architects (established in 1981).

Wide-ranging in its scope and abilities, Quadrangle has assembled a portfolio of projects that illustrates a diverse client list and expertise in a wide variety of building types from corporate to commercial to residential. In addition to its many large-scale projects, Quadrangle is highly regarded for its corporate and commercial interiors carried out by its in-house interior design group.

LORNE GERTNER

Mr. Gertner is a serial entrepreneur with experience in start-ups, going public transactions, fashion, retail, architecture, real estate, finance and cannabis. Mr. Gertner is a co-founder and former chairman of PharmaCan Capital Corporation (MMJ.V) (now Cronos) Canada’s first publicly traded merchant bank in the medical marijuana sector with investments in five of the thirty-eight legal licensed producers in Canada. He is also a cofounder of Tokyo Smoke. Mr. Gertner was a cofounder and director of Cannasat Pharmaceuticals Inc. (CYNA) The first medical marijuana company to be listed on the Toronto stock exchange with an investment in the first government contracted facility growing medical marijuana in Canada called Prairie Plant Systems now called Cannimed Therapeutics Inc. (CMED.TO).

Mr. Gertner is the current Chairman of HG2 Capital Corp., an investment / merchant bank in the cannabis sector, and is the CEO of Hill and Gertner Capital Corp. He concurrently serves on the boards of Emblem Corp. (EMC.V), Green Acre Capital and the Design Exchange. He is also an advance professor at the Daniel School of Architecture Landscape and Urban Design at The University of Toronto.

Mr. Gertner is a graduate of The John H. Daniels Faculty of Architecture, University of Toronto and has completed the ICD.D designation at the Rotman School of Management.

Cynapsus

Cynapsus is a specialty pharmaceutical company developing a convenient and easy to use sublingual (oral) thin film strip for the acute rescue of “off” motor symptoms of Parkinson’s disease. Over one million people in the U.S. and an estimated 5 million people globally suffer from Parkinson's disease. Parkinson’s disease is a chronic and progressive neurodegenerative disease that impacts motor activity, and its prevalence is increasing with the aging of the population.
